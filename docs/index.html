Community Real-Estate Agency

ISIC Rev.5 6810 — Open Business Blueprint
The problem: Real-estate closings require human judgment and legal accountability, but operators often build compliance from scratch. The LLM can draft disclosures and spot issues, but it has no legal standing and no business being the one that decides whether a title transfer happens.
The solution: An independent RealtorGovernor seals the LLM into a single node and wraps it with human approval workflows, jurisdiction-specific enforcement gates, and an immutable audit ledger. The governor holds unsafe commits. No autonomous recordings. No silent liability.

What It Does

  • Governed closing execution: Intake, jurisdiction-specific checklists, KYC-sanctions screening, and human-approval gates
  • Parcel & listing management: Link properties to listings, validate term overlaps, manage active leases
  • Tenancy & transaction records: Complete audit trail for rent, maintenance, handovers, and every state change
  • Audit ledger: Immutable record of every proposal, hold, and decision

What It Is Not

It does not hold a broker/agent license. You supply the jurisdiction-specific license, the real KYC/AML program, land-registry integrations, and escrow connections. You bear that jurisdiction's liability; the software supplies the governed execution scaffold so you don't build the compliance layer from scratch.
