Community Real-Estate Agency

ISIC Rev.5 6810 — Open Business Blueprint

An open-source, forkable real-estate agency platform for independent property operators, housing cooperatives, and community land trusts. Manage listings, leases, tenancy records, and close sales with full transparency.

Built on the Realtor-LLM ⊣ RealtorGovernor actor pattern: an LLM advisor proposes closings and disclosures, while an independent governor enforces jurisdiction-specific requirements, screens for sanctions, and gates final execution to human approval. No autonomous recordings. No silent liability.

Deploy, run, and extend it in your jurisdiction. Fork on GitHub, self-host on your infrastructure, and sell the service to licensed operators. Governance, social impact metrics, and audit trails built in from the start.

Key Features

  • Governed closing execution: Intake, jurisdiction-specific disclosure checklists, KYC-sanctions screening, human approval, and audit ledger
  • Parcel & listing management: Attach properties to listings; validate term overlaps; manage active leases
  • Tenancy records: Track rent, maintenance, handovers, and audit trails for every transaction
  • Role-based access: Agent, operator, and management views with immutable audit logs
  • Jurisdiction-specific facts: Extensible catalog of disclosure requirements with official spec-based citations (6 jurisdictions seeded)

What It Is (and Isn't)

  • It governs closing workflows: Listing intake, disclosure checklists, KYC screening, closing proposals, and human-approved recording gates
  • It does not hold a broker/agent license. You supply the jurisdiction-specific license, the real KYC/AML program, and the land-registry / escrow integrations. You bear that jurisdiction's liability; the software supplies the governed execution scaffold.
  • It never records a closing or disburses escrow autonomously. Two independent layers enforce this: the governor's actuation gate and the phase table. A human operator always signs off.
